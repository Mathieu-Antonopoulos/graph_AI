How Diffusion Works

The heat variation at each node follows the Discrete Laplace Equation:

$$\frac{dT}{dt} = -\alpha \mathbf{L} T$$

The Laplacian matrix $\mathbf{L}$ is defined as $\mathbf{D} - \mathbf{A}$. It computes the local energy balance for each node $i$:

$$(LT)_i = \underbrace{d_i T_i}_{\text{Outgoing}} - \underbrace{\sum_{j \sim i} T_j}_{\text{Incoming}}$$
  • Outgoing ($D$): The node distributes its heat to its $d_i$ neighbors.
  • Incoming ($-A$): The node absorbs heat from its connected neighbors.

Where $T$ is the temperature vector containing the state of all nodes at time $t$:

$$T(t) = \begin{bmatrix} T_1 \\ T_2 \\ \vdots \\ T_n \end{bmatrix}$$

Each component $T_i$ represents the temperature of a specific room or outdoor area (Kitchen, Garden, etc.).

Note: If $(LT)_i$ is positive, the node is hotter than its neighbors' average and will cool down (since the update is $-\alpha LT$).
